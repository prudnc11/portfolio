Taking a look at some of the old designs

Biggest pain points for many Boxcare users
mainly existed from the request process :

Before we jumped into the project, our team read through some of the reviews and feedback that customers were able to provide us with the old version of the Boxwash request process. A lot of pain points lived within the confusions of selecting their payments and the whole journey to request a simple car required a very long process.

The first step we took was to become one of the users to test every aspect and observe the steps that I needed to take to reach my objective. From this step, we were able to find interactions and processes where users were most confused and to see where the most pains occurred throughout the objective. We also conducted user testings with anyone that owned a car + wash and fueling experiences to gather feedback.

From our previously gathered feedback and user testings, we were able to summarize some of the major problems that lived inside the request process.

Key takeaways

From several user testings and going through each different use cases,
we were able to catch some amazing key takeaways from this process.

1. Bottom navigation distractions

During the request process, Norton got off the path due to the bottom NAV that was distracting her from the process. This was crucial because it went to a different screen that lost the user's focus from requesting a wash.

2. Size of buttons and text

The text sizes were way too small for Norton to read, tap buttons were misleading and confusing and did not find where to proceed with the payment options.

3. Complexity

Norton needed guidance and help throughout the process of making a wash request. The experience was too complex and not easy to digest.

4. Unnecessary Steps

Customers needed to go through many steps to reach the summary page which was overwhelming and unnecessary.

5. Consistency with designs

There were many new features within one page that users did not understand. Buttons were misleading and different in style which caused confusion during the process.

6. Need saved orders or favorites

Customers needed saved addresses or orders to reduce the number of steps that they would need to take for their next order.

Design requirements
Simplicity

Help and lead users seamlessly to request a car wash and fuel services. It should include additional pieces of information with different services and pricing if needed. Anyone should be able to reach their objectives without any questions.

Speed

Decrease the unnecessary steps with going back and forth with selections that needed to be made during the request. This was one of our main issues because users wished to achieve their wash and fuel service quickly.

Aim for general usability

A designer should cover the majority of use cases regardless of age and gender. Boxcare needs to be inclusive to a wide user audience.

Final touches and delivery

Boxwash new designs

The simplified and quicker request flow for our current and potential customers was our main objective. Also, aiming for general usability was very important to aim for a wider range of customers inside the Bay Area. With many rounds of user testings and revisions, we were able to bring a cohesive design system for Boxwash and here is what we have delivered and shipped!

To summarize, let's take a look at what major changes has happened :

Simplicity

The design is intuitive and very simple to use for general users today.

User friendly

Customers will be guided throughout the whole process to follow step by step.

Steps

Reduced the number of pages and steps that the user needed to take to make a request.

Design?

Button styles are cohesive visually and the features are very easy to digest.


New IA map

Simplified and improved IA map

Comparing the two IA maps from before and after, we changed a lot of steps and structure for the request process to be better, faster and more efficient. The steps that customers needed to take reduced by half amount thanks to the drawers feature that has been added into our new design system. The users are well guided throughout the whole process from start to finish without any distractions or feeling overwhelmed by all the misguided buttons.

We were able to successfully ship this updated version and continued working on some of the new features that were missing to make continuous improvements and meet the user needs.
